Buying Guide

When choosing the best home generator for power outages in Massachusetts, there are several key features to look for:

* Power output: The power output of a generator is measured in watts and determines how many appliances you can power at once. A higher power output is generally better, but it also increases the price and size of the generator. * Engine size: The engine size of a generator determines its power output and durability. A larger engine size generally produces more power and is more durable, but it also increases the price and size of the generator. * Fuel type: Generators can run on gasoline, propane, or natural gas. Gasoline is the most common fuel type, but propane and natural gas are more efficient and produce fewer emissions. * Noise level: The noise level of a generator is measured in decibels and determines how loud it is. A lower noise level is generally better, especially for residential use. * Portability: The portability of a generator determines how easy it is to move and transport. A more portable generator is generally better, especially for RV use or small homes. * Safety features: Generators can be equipped with safety features such as automatic shutdown, overload protection, and ground fault circuit interrupter (GFCI) protection. These features help prevent accidents and ensure safe operation.

When buying a home generator, there are also several red flags to avoid:

* Low-quality construction: A low-quality construction can lead to premature wear and tear, reducing the lifespan of the generator. * Lack of safety features: A lack of safety features can increase the risk of accidents and injuries. * Poor customer support: Poor customer support can make it difficult to get help when you need it, especially during an emergency. * Low warranty: A low warranty can leave you with little protection in case the generator fails or needs repairs.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about home generators:

* What size generator do I need?: The size of the generator you need depends on the number and type of appliances you want to power. A general rule of thumb is to calculate the total wattage of the appliances you want to power and add 10-20% to account for startup surges. * How long can I run a generator?: The runtime of a generator depends on the fuel tank size and the power output. A larger fuel tank and a lower power output can increase the runtime of the generator. * Can I use a generator indoors?: No, generators should never be used indoors due to the risk of carbon monoxide poisoning. Always use a generator outdoors, at least 20 feet away from any windows or doors. * Do I need a transfer switch?: A transfer switch is required to connect a generator to your home's electrical panel. It helps to automatically switch between the generator and the utility power, ensuring safe and efficient operation.
