E-commerce Store Tracker Template

The E-commerce Store Tracker template is a comprehensive tool designed to help online store owners manage their sales, inventory, and customer data in one place. This template includes multiple sheets to track different aspects of your e-commerce business.

1. Sheet Names (Tabs) Needed:

1. Dashboard: A summary sheet that provides an overview of your store's performance. 2. Orders: A sheet to track individual orders, including customer information and order details. 3. Products: A sheet to manage your product catalog, including inventory levels and pricing. 4. Customers: A sheet to store customer information and order history. 5. Inventory: A sheet to track inventory levels and manage stock replenishment. 6. Sales: A sheet to analyze sales data, including revenue and profit margins. 7. Marketing: A sheet to track marketing campaigns and their effectiveness.

2. Column Headers for Each Sheet with Data Types:

Orders Sheet:

1. Order ID (Text): Unique identifier for each order. 2. Date (Date): Date the order was placed. 3. Customer Name (Text): Name of the customer who placed the order. 4. Email (Text): Customer's email address. 5. Order Total (Number): Total value of the order. 6. Status (Text): Status of the order (e.g., "Pending", "Shipped", "Delivered"). 7. Product (Text): Product(s) ordered. 8. Quantity (Number): Quantity of each product ordered.

Products Sheet:

1. Product ID (Text): Unique identifier for each product. 2. Product Name (Text): Name of the product. 3. Description (Text): Brief description of the product. 4. Price (Number): Price of the product. 5. Inventory (Number): Current inventory level of the product. 6. Category (Text): Category of the product (e.g., "Electronics", "Clothing").

Customers Sheet:

1. Customer ID (Text): Unique identifier for each customer. 2. Name (Text): Name of the customer. 3. Email (Text): Customer's email address. 4. Order History (Text): List of orders placed by the customer. 5. Total Spend (Number): Total amount spent by the customer.

Inventory Sheet:

1. Product ID (Text): Unique identifier for each product. 2. Quantity (Number): Current inventory level of the product. 3. Reorder Point (Number): Minimum inventory level that triggers a reorder. 4. Reorder Quantity (Number): Quantity to reorder when the inventory level falls below the reorder point.

Sales Sheet:

1. Date (Date): Date of the sale. 2. Product (Text): Product sold. 3. Quantity (Number): Quantity sold. 4. Revenue (Number): Total revenue from the sale. 5. Profit (Number): Profit from the sale.

Marketing Sheet:

1. Campaign ID (Text): Unique identifier for each marketing campaign. 2. Campaign Name (Text): Name of the marketing campaign. 3. Date (Date): Date the campaign was launched. 4. Budget (Number): Budget allocated for the campaign. 5. Results (Text): Results of the campaign (e.g., "Click-through rate", "Conversion rate").

3. Key Formulas:

* Total Sales: SUM of all sales in the Sales sheet. * Inventory Level: SUM of all quantities in the Inventory sheet. * Customer Lifetime Value: AVERAGE of total spend by customers in the Customers sheet. * Order Fulfillment Rate: COUNT of orders with status "Delivered" divided by total number of orders. * Marketing ROI: Revenue generated by a marketing campaign divided by the campaign budget.

4. Color Coding Scheme:

* Green: Used for positive values (e.g., revenue, profit). * Red: Used for negative values (e.g., losses, declined orders). * Yellow: Used for neutral values (e.g., pending orders, inventory levels). * Blue: Used for headers and titles.

5. Dropdown Options for Key Fields:

* Status (Orders sheet): "Pending", "Shipped", "Delivered", "Cancelled". * Category (Products sheet): "Electronics", "Clothing", "Home Goods", "Toys". * Campaign Type (Marketing sheet): "Email", "Social Media", "Paid Advertising", "Influencer Marketing".

6. Instructions Section:

To use this template, follow these steps:

1. Enter your sales data into the Orders sheet. 2. Update your product catalog in the Products sheet. 3. Manage your customer data in the Customers sheet. 4. Track your inventory levels in the Inventory sheet. 5. Analyze your sales data in the Sales sheet. 6. Plan and track your marketing campaigns in the Marketing sheet. 7. Use the Dashboard sheet to get an overview of your store's performance.
