Small Business Budget Tracker Template

This comprehensive template is designed to help small business owners track their income and expenses, stay on top of their finances, and make informed decisions. The template consists of 7 sheets (tabs) that work together to provide a complete picture of your business's financial health.

1. Sheet Names (Tabs):

1. Dashboard: A summary sheet that provides an overview of your business's financial performance. 2. Income: A sheet to track all income sources, including sales, services, and other revenue streams. 3. Fixed Expenses: A sheet to track regular, fixed expenses such as rent, utilities, and salaries. 4. Variable Expenses: A sheet to track expenses that vary from month to month, such as marketing, travel, and supplies. 5. Accounts Payable: A sheet to track outstanding bills and invoices. 6. Accounts Receivable: A sheet to track outstanding payments from customers. 7. Settings: A sheet to store settings, such as tax rates and currency symbols.

3. Key Formulas:

* Total Income: SUM of all income amounts in the Income sheet. * Total Fixed Expenses: SUM of all fixed expense amounts in the Fixed Expenses sheet. * Total Variable Expenses: SUM of all variable expense amounts in the Variable Expenses sheet. * Net Income: Total Income - (Total Fixed Expenses + Total Variable Expenses). * Accounts Payable Balance: SUM of all outstanding invoice amounts in the Accounts Payable sheet. * Accounts Receivable Balance: SUM of all outstanding invoice amounts in the Accounts Receivable sheet.

4. Color Coding Scheme:

* Income: Green * Fixed Expenses: Red * Variable Expenses: Yellow * Accounts Payable: Orange * Accounts Receivable: Blue

5. Dropdown Options for Key Fields:

* Income Type: Sales, Services, Interest, Other * Expense Type: Rent, Utilities, Salaries, Marketing, Travel, Supplies, Other * Category: Sales, Marketing, Operations, Administration, Other * Frequency: Monthly, Quarterly, Annually * Status: Paid, Pending, Overdue

6. Instructions Section:

1. Enter all income sources in the Income sheet, including sales, services, and other revenue streams. 2. Enter all fixed expenses in the Fixed Expenses sheet, including rent, utilities, and salaries. 3. Enter all variable expenses in the Variable Expenses sheet, including marketing, travel, and supplies. 4. Enter all outstanding bills and invoices in the Accounts Payable sheet. 5. Enter all outstanding payments from customers in the Accounts Receivable sheet. 6. Review the Dashboard sheet regularly to track your business's financial performance.
