Real Estate Deal Analyzer Template

The Real Estate Deal Analyzer template is a comprehensive tool designed to help real estate investors and professionals analyze and compare potential deals. The template consists of multiple sheets, each with its own set of column headers, formulas, and formatting.

1. Sheet Names (Tabs) Needed:

1. Deal Summary: A summary of all deals, including key metrics and calculations. 2. Deal Details: A detailed breakdown of each deal, including property information, financials, and assumptions. 3. Cash Flow: A cash flow analysis of each deal, including income, expenses, and net operating income. 4. Sensitivity Analysis: A sensitivity analysis of each deal, including variables such as interest rates, rental income, and expenses. 5. Instructions: A sheet with instructions on how to use the template and explanations of key formulas and concepts.

2. Column Headers for Each Sheet with Data Types:

Deal Summary:

1. Deal ID (text): Unique identifier for each deal. 2. Property Address (text): Address of the property. 3. Purchase Price (number): Purchase price of the property. 4. Rental Income (number): Annual rental income. 5. Expenses (number): Annual expenses, including property management, maintenance, and taxes. 6. Net Operating Income (NOI) (number): Annual NOI, calculated as rental income minus expenses. 7. Cap Rate (number): Capitalization rate, calculated as NOI divided by purchase price. 8. Cash on Cash Return (number): Cash on cash return, calculated as annual cash flow divided by initial investment.

Deal Details:

1. Property Type (text): Type of property (e.g., single-family home, apartment building, etc.). 2. Number of Units (number): Number of rental units. 3. Square Footage (number): Total square footage of the property. 4. Age of Property (number): Age of the property in years. 5. Condition of Property (text): Condition of the property (e.g., excellent, good, fair, poor). 6. Financing Terms (text): Financing terms, including interest rate, loan term, and loan amount. 7. Closing Costs (number): Closing costs, including title insurance, appraisal fee, and other costs. 8. Renovation Costs (number): Renovation costs, including materials, labor, and permits.

Cash Flow:

1. Monthly Rental Income (number): Monthly rental income. 2. Monthly Expenses (number): Monthly expenses, including property management, maintenance, and taxes. 3. Monthly Net Operating Income (number): Monthly NOI, calculated as rental income minus expenses. 4. Annual Cash Flow (number): Annual cash flow, calculated as monthly NOI multiplied by 12. 5. Initial Investment (number): Initial investment, including down payment, closing costs, and renovation costs.

Sensitivity Analysis:

1. Interest Rate (number): Interest rate used in financing calculations. 2. Rental Income Growth Rate (number): Annual growth rate of rental income. 3. Expense Growth Rate (number): Annual growth rate of expenses. 4. Cap Rate (number): Capitalization rate used in valuation calculations. 5. Cash on Cash Return (number): Cash on cash return, calculated as annual cash flow divided by initial investment.

3. Key Formulas (Written as Text Descriptions):

* Net Operating Income (NOI): Rental income minus expenses. * Cap Rate: NOI divided by purchase price. * Cash on Cash Return: Annual cash flow divided by initial investment. * Annual Cash Flow: Monthly NOI multiplied by 12. * Initial Investment: Down payment plus closing costs plus renovation costs.

4. Color Coding Scheme:

* Green: Positive cash flow, high cap rate, and high cash on cash return. * Yellow: Neutral cash flow, average cap rate, and average cash on cash return. * Red: Negative cash flow, low cap rate, and low cash on cash return.

5. Dropdown Options for Key Fields:

* Property Type: Single-family home, apartment building, condominium, townhouse, etc. * Financing Terms: Cash, conventional loan, FHA loan, VA loan, etc. * Condition of Property: Excellent, good, fair, poor.

6. Instructions Section:

The instructions section provides a step-by-step guide on how to use the template, including:

* Entering deal information into the Deal Details sheet. * Reviewing and analyzing the Deal Summary sheet. * Using the Cash Flow sheet to calculate annual cash flow. * Using the Sensitivity Analysis sheet to test different scenarios. * Interpreting the results and making informed investment decisions.
